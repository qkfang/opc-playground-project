## Objective
CPS 230 requires an APRA-regulated entity to effectively manage its operational risks, maintain its
critical operations within board-approved tolerance levels through severe disruptions, and manage the
risks arising from the use of service providers. It consolidates and replaces a number of earlier
standards, including CPS 231 Outsourcing and CPS 232 Business Continuity Management.

## Roles and responsibilities (governance)
- The Board is ultimately accountable for the oversight of operational risk management.
- The entity must clearly define the roles and responsibilities of the Board, senior management and
  the risk and control functions for operational risk, business continuity and the management of
  service providers.
- Senior management is responsible for implementing the operational risk management framework.

## Operational risk management (framework)
- Maintain an operational risk management framework appropriate to the size, business mix and
  complexity of the entity.
- Identify and assess operational risks arising from people, processes, systems, data and external
  events, including from material changes and new products/systems.
- Monitor the operational risk profile against risk appetite and report to the Board and senior
  management.
- Remediate operational risk control weaknesses and deficiencies in a timely manner.

## Operational risk controls
- Design, implement and embed internal controls to manage operational risks.
- Regularly assess the design and operating effectiveness of controls, including controls operated by
  service providers.
- Monitor controls and obtain assurance over their effectiveness; escalate and remediate failures.

## Business continuity and operational resilience
- Maintain the ability to continue critical operations within tolerance levels through severe
  disruptions.
- The Board must approve tolerance levels for each critical operation, expressed as the maximum level
  of disruption the entity is willing to accept (for example, maximum tolerable downtime, maximum data
  loss, and minimum service levels).
- Identify and maintain the people, processes, technology, facilities and information required to
  operate within tolerance, and understand interdependencies and concentrations.

## Critical operations
- Identify critical operations and maintain a register, reviewed at least annually.
- Critical operations are processes which, if disrupted beyond tolerance, would have a material adverse
  impact on depositors, policyholders, beneficiaries or the financial system.
- Map the resources supporting each critical operation and assess the impact of disruption.

## Management of service providers
- Maintain a Board-approved service provider management policy covering the end-to-end lifecycle.
- Maintain a register of material service providers and material arrangements.
- Conduct due diligence before entering into a material arrangement and monitor providers on an
  ongoing basis.
- Ensure formal, legally binding agreements are in place for material arrangements, covering audit and
  access rights, sub-contracting, and the ability to meet the entity's obligations.
- Assess concentration risk and the risks arising from a service provider's own material
  sub-contractors (fourth parties).

## Incident and disruption management
- Identify, escalate, record, respond to and learn from operational risk incidents.
- Manage disruptions to critical operations to restore them within tolerance levels.

## Business continuity plan (BCP)
- Maintain a BCP that sets out how the entity will maintain critical operations within tolerance
  through disruptions, including the resources, actions and arrangements required.

## Scenario testing
- Conduct scenario testing of the ability to maintain critical operations within tolerance under a
  range of severe but plausible scenarios.
- Review and test the BCP at least annually and address deficiencies identified.

## Notifications to APRA
- Notify APRA as soon as possible and no later than 72 hours after becoming aware of an operational
  risk incident that is likely to have a material financial impact or a material impact on the ability
  to maintain critical operations.
- Notify APRA prior to entering into, or on becoming aware of a material change to, a material service
  provider arrangement.
- Notify APRA of disruptions that result in a critical operation operating outside tolerance.
